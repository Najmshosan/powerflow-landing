Account structure

Separate journeys for investors and consumers

Your registration flow should clearly separate these two user types because they join the business for different reasons, under different commercial and legal arrangements.

🏠

Consumer Account

Designed for households, tenants, offices, hostels, shops, SMEs and estate users that want reliable power access through Trefcel Energy.

  • Register for a smart meter account
  • Pay the registration fee
  • Complete agreement acceptance before payment
  • Request installation and activation
  • Top up power once the meter is active
  • Potentially buy electricity supplied from investor-owned assets
📈

Investor Account

Designed for individuals, diaspora buyers, cooperatives, institutions and businesses that want to own solar assets and earn from the power they produce.

  • Buy assets outright or on approved instalment
  • Retain ownership rights under signed platform terms
  • Monitor asset performance and energy utilisation
  • Sell excess generation to approved consumers
  • Expand into multiple energy assets over time
  • Participate in future EV charging infrastructure ownership

Onboarding flow

Registration page structure for deployment

The registration page should be long-form and clear, with distinct sections for Consumer Registration and Investor Registration.

Consumer Registration Fields

  • Full name, phone, email and premises address
  • Property type and energy usage type
  • Whether AC or inverter AC usage is expected
  • Wiring readiness and electrician arrangement
  • Agreement acceptance before payment
  • Meter request and installation scheduling

Investor Registration Fields

  • Full name, phone, email, KYC and address information
  • Preferred investment mode: outright or instalment
  • Preferred solar product or asset class
  • Deployment location and ownership purpose
  • Own-use, resale-use or hybrid-use selection
  • Acceptance of investor ownership and energy settlement terms

Terms, ownership and installation conditions

These terms should be shown clearly before checkout and agreement acceptance.

Consumer Terms

  • Consumers can still power appliances including AC, with inverter AC preferred for efficiency.
  • A registration fee applies before activation.
  • The customer is responsible for wiring from the main line to the office or premises.
  • The customer discusses electrician workmanship and labour cost directly with the electrician.
  • Trefcel Energy provides the smart meter, changeover switch and electrical breaker free under service terms.
  • These supplied items remain the property of Trefcel Energy.
  • Replacement is free for qualifying faults where there is no customer damage or tampering.
  • The solar customer agreement must be signed before payment.

Investor Terms

  • The investor is the owner of the qualifying asset purchased from Trefcel Energy, whether bought outright or on approved instalment.
  • Ownership is subject to signed platform and asset-use agreements.
  • Trefcel Energy may manage installation, monitoring, metering and consumer allocation under agreed operational terms.
  • Investors may sell excess power to connected consumers where technically and contractually approved.
  • Settlement, maintenance, repossession on instalment default, operating limits and revenue rights should be governed by the signed investor agreement.
Damage, tampering, bypassing, unauthorised rewiring, meter interference, or misuse of supplied equipment may void replacement support and may result in suspension, enforcement action or recovery under the signed agreement.
